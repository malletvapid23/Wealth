Today we see a lot of progress around the World. Our technologies are advancing at an accelareted rate. Allowing us to communicate instantly, get quickly organized, make collective decisions and now even make crowd investments. As communities, our capabilities are getting better at solving problems. On the other side, we still have big challenges like economic crises, destruction of the environment, income inequality and so on. It's a good time to question existing systems, improve them by the new possibilities and try to deal with these global issues.

One area that can be questioned is how the economic system works and how we distribute our resources.

Economic system is responsible for allocating the resources to the organizations, most commonly via the transactions between the organizations and the consumers. To get more resources, an organization has to;

  • Maximize the price, therefore the profit
  • Increase the number of sales
However, organizations have also other features that can be measured and equally important for the society; like responsibilities towards its workers, being environment friendly or even producing healthy products.

An unknown profiting object. It gets more resources regardless of how and what sort of product or service it is providing to the society.

Wealth Economy is an attempt to create a collabrative, customizable system to allow the society to manage its resources. It aims to define the various features of the organizations that important and beneficial for the society, measure the overall performance of the organizations on these features and allocate the resources based on the outcome.

Sector Index: The Big Picture

One of the issues in the current system is that the consumers mostly only interact with the organizations about their daily needs.

For example, one might think that education sector is more important for the society than most of the other sectors. However, to be able to interact and spend money within this sector, one should either be a student or a relative of a student. Otherwise, even though it is still important, it would be out of the routine and cannot be supported in a regular basis. Instead, one could spend her income on clothing, cosmetics, entertainment and so on, that might actually have a less priority.

Sector Index could gives us the opportunity to see the overall of the system and let us distribute the resources to the sectors by comparing them.

In the next part, you will see 6 important sectors. Every organization under these sectors is trying to solve one of the issues that the society has. One is developing video games to entertain, another is trying to educate, one is trying to cure cancer, another is trying to make you look good. We believe, when we could directly choose which sectors have more priority and could affect how to distribute the resources, the result would be very different than the current one.

Knowledge Index: Global Knowledge Database

While creating new products and services, organizations also produce the knowledge of how these products, services could be created. All sorts of intellectual property, like discoveries, inventions, formulas; music, literature, and other artistic works; software source codes, and designs could be counted as such knowledge.

Although this is a very unique and important asset to the society, majority of the organizations tend to keep this knowledge to themselves by using restricted licenses, patents. In the long term, this 'overprotection' turns out to be a very counter-productive approach, reduces the collabration chance between the organizations. When the knowledge was not shared, every other organization has to spend a similar time and energy to get to the same level. In other words, we keep reinventing the same knowledge over and over again.

Rewarding the organizations that use licenses with more flexible rights could encourage sharing the knowledge and let us build a common knowledge.

Knowledge Index allows us to compare the licenses side by side and rate them directly. The resources then could be distributed to the organizations based on their license choice.

In the next part, you will see two sample licenses that represent the existing situation in general. Open source licenses usually give much more right to the users of its product and restricted licenses, mostly used for propritary products, would only allow to the product to be used by its owner.

On the contrary to this situation, in reality, from income perspective, restricted licenses dominate their market. In other words, we reward the organizations that use restricted licenses.

If we could encourage the organizations to share their knowledge and let them cooperate, we could save massive time & energy and start building a common knowledge.
