Today we see a lot of progress around the World. Our technologies are advancing at an accelareted rate. Allowing us to communicate instantly, get quickly organized, make collective decisions and now even make crowd investments. As communities, our capabilities are getting better at solving problems. On the other side, we still have big challenges like economic crises, destruction of the environment, income inequality and so on. It's a good time to question existing systems, improve them by the new possibilities and try to deal with these global issues.

One area that can be questioned is how the economic system works and how we distribute our resources.

Economic system is responsible for allocating the resources to the organizations, most commonly via the transactions between the organizations and the consumers. To get more resources, an organization has to;

  • Maximize the price, therefore the profit
  • Increase the number of sales
However, organizations have also other features that can be measured and equally important for the society; like responsibilities towards its workers, being environment friendly or even producing healthy products.

An unknown profiting object. It gets more resources regardless of how and what sort of product or service it is providing to the society.

Wealth Economy is an attempt to create a collabrative, customizable system to allow the society to manage its resources. It aims to define the various features of the organizations that important and beneficial for the society, measure the overall performance of the organizations on these features and allocate the resources based on the outcome.

Sector Index: The Big Picture

One of the issues in the current system is that the consumers only interact with the organizations about their regular / daily needs.

For example, the consumer could think that education sector is more important for the society than most of the other sectors. However, if she is not a student or a parent of a student, she has no direct relation with this sector, therefore cannot support it in a systematic way. Instead, she could spend her resources on clothing, cosmetics, entertainment and so on, that might have a less priority.

This is one of the reasons why education system is explicitly supported by the states in even if the country is using Free Market economy.

Sector Index gives us the opportunity to see the overall picture and distribute the resources to the sectors by comparing them.
