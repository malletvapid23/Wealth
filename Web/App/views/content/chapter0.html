Wealth Economy

An experimental resource management system that aims to provide a sustainable economic model.

Introduction

Today we see a lot of progress around the World. Our technologies are advancing at an accelareted rate. Allowing us to communicate instantly, get quickly organized, make collective decisions and now even make crowd investments. As communities, our capabilities are getting better at solving problems. On the other side, we still have big challenges like economic crises, destruction of the environment, income inequality and so on. It's a good time to question existing systems, improve them by the new possibilities and try to deal with these global issues.

One area that can be questioned is how the economic system works and how we distribute our resources.

Economic system is responsible for allocating the resources to the organizations, most commonly via the transactions between the organizations and the consumers. To get more resources, an organization has to;

  • Maximize the price, therefore the profit
  • Increase the number of sales
However, organizations have also other features that can be measured and equally important for the society; like responsibilities towards its workers, being environment friendly or even producing healthy products.

Wealth Economy is an attempt to create a collabrative, customizable system to allow the society manage its resources. It aims to define various parameters for the organizations that are beneficial for the society, measure the organizations' overall performance on these parameters and allocate the resources based on the outcome.

Sector Index: The Big Picture

One of the issues in the current system is that the consumers usually only interact with the organizations about their daily needs.

For example, one might think that education sector is more important for the society than most of the other sectors. However, to be able to interact and make transactions with this sector, one should either be a student or a relative of a student. Otherwise, it would be out of the routine and cannot be supported in a regular basis. Instead, one could spend its income on clothing, cosmetics, entertainment and so on, that might have a less priority.

Sector Index could gives us the opportunity to see the overall of the system and let us distribute the resources to the sectors by comparing them.

In the next part, you will see six important sectors. Every organization under these sectors is trying to solve one of the issues that the society has. One is developing video games to entertain, another is trying to educate, one is trying to cure cancer, another is trying to make you look good. We believe, when we could directly choose which sectors have more priority and could affect how to distribute the resources, the result would be very different than the current one.

Knowledge Index: Global Knowledge Database

While creating new products and services, organizations also produce the knowledge of how these products, services could be created. All sorts of intellectual property, like discoveries, inventions, formulas; music, literature, and other artistic works; software source codes, and designs could be counted as such knowledge.

Although this is a very unique and important asset to the society, majority of the organizations tend to keep this knowledge to themselves by using restricted licenses, patents. In the long term, this 'overprotection' turns out to be a very counter-productive approach, reduces the collabration chance between the organizations. When the knowledge has not shared, every other organization has to spend a similar time and energy to get to the same level. In other words, we keep reinventing the same knowledge over and over again.

Rewarding the organizations that use licenses with more flexible rights could encourage sharing the knowledge and let us build a common knowledge.

Knowledge Index allows us to compare the parents, licenses side by side and rate them directly. The resources then could be distributed to the organizations based on their license choice.

In the next part, you will see two sample licenses that represent the existing situation in general. Open source licenses usually give much more right to the users of its product and restricted licenses, mostly used for proprietary products, would only allow the product to be used by its owner.

In reality, from income perspective, restricted licenses dominate their market. In other words, we reward the organizations that use restricted licenses.

If we could encourage the organizations to share their knowledge and let them cooperate, we could save massive time & energy and start building a common knowledge.

Total Cost Index: Profit - Nonprofit Paradox

In the current system, more profit means more resources for the organization. Therefore majority of the organizations aim to maximize their profits. However, what is beneficial for the organization is exactly the opposite for the society. Trying to maximize the profit means that the organization becomes more expensive to society in solving the issues.

In the following sample, there are three different prices for the same product. Although No Profit case solves the issue in most effective way, because of "more expensive means more resources" rule, High Profit case will always have bring more resources to its organization.

With this logic, we allocate limited resources to the organizations that have less cost on the society and keep encouraging the organizations to maximize their profit. This is one of the paradoxes of the current system.

Our solution for this problem could be high debatable and may not be the right approach, however we believe this issue worth to be discussed, addressed and hopefully solved.

With Total Cost Index, Sales Price itself used as the organization's rating in terms of "Cost to the Society". Different than the previous samples, with this index, high number means low performance and low income from the pool.

Resource pool amount

As in this sample, if we could manage to allocate more resources to the lowest cost case, then aiming for the maximum price approach would become obsolete. Then what should be expected is that the organizations would compete with each other to have the lowest price on for their products.

Fair Share Index: Sharers vs. Keepers

Most of the organizations in the market uses the classic capitalistic approach; the business owner makes the initial investment while creating the business and sets the rules in favor of himself, most importantly like getting all the profit. This means that even after years of work and significant contributions, most of the employees will not get a share from organization's income but get a fixed salary. In the long term, since the organization owners keep getting a bigger pie from the income and not sharing it accordingly, this type of income distribution becomes one of the root causes of "Income Inequality".

It's important to note that if an organization does otherwise, share the income with its employees in a fair way, in the current system, this wouldn't have any financial benefit to that organization. At best, the organization could include this difference in their marketing strategy and try to communicate with its consumer about this detail, which may or may not increase their sales number.

By using Fair Share Index, we can categorize the organizations into "Sharers" vs. "Keepers". Again we can decide which side is more important by rating the options, then the organizations will get an income from the pool based on their approach.
