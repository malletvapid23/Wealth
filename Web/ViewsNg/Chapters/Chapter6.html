#6 Total Cost Index: Profit - Nonprofit Paradox

 
« Previous Chapter Next Chapter »

In the current system, since that’s the only way to get more resources, majority of the organizations aim to maximize their profits. However, what is beneficial for the organization is exactly the opposite for the society. Trying to maximize the profit means that the organization becomes more expensive to society in solving the issues.

In the following sample, there are two organizations that produce the same type of product with the same features. The only difference is their Sales Price.

Organization "Lowlands" solves the issue in a more cost-effective way the other organization. However, since currently being more expensive also brings more resources, at the end of the race, "High Coast" will always have more resources than its competitor.

With this logic, we will keep encouring the organizations to maximize their profit and allocate less resources to the organizations that have a less cost on the society. This is one of the paradoxes that we are having in the current system.

New target: Profit minimization

Total Cost Index aims to balance this situation by calculating the difference between the sales prices and allocates the difference to the most cost-effective organizations as a compensation.

Please choose the compansation level for Total Cost Index. Sales Price will be multiplied by this percentage.

In the current system, since that’s the only way to get more resources, majority of the organizations primary target is to maximize their profits. However, this actually means that the organization gets more expensive to society in solving the issues. This problem is one of the reasons that we have “Inequality” in the system.

If we can manage to create a system that helps us to support the organizations that can solve the issues with less cost to the society, this may lead to a change in profit oriented structure and can reduce the gap between the rich and poor.

Organization “1x” solves the problem (produces and sells 100 phones) with the minimum possible cost (total revenue equals to total production cost). Organization “4x” solves the same problem with four times higher cost. Although “1x” shows a better performance in solving the problem (total cost to society is lesser), at the end of the race, “4x” will gain more resources than its competitor. This is one of the paradoxes that we are having in the current system.
